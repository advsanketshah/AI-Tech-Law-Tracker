      <h4 class="entry-title">EU Commission Proposes AI Act Simplification - High-Risk Rules Delayed to December 2027</h4>
      <div class="entry-meta">
        <span class="meta-badge source">ğŸ“„ European Commission</span>
        <span class="meta-badge jurisdiction">ğŸ‡ªğŸ‡º European Union</span>
        <span class="meta-badge date">ğŸ“… Nov 19, 2025</span>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="entry-section">
      <div class="entry-section-label">What Happened</div>
      <div class="entry-section-content">
        European Commission proposes "Digital Omnibus" package delaying high-risk AI system requirements until December 2, 2027 (from August 2, 2026). Delays linked to slow designation of conformity assessment bodies and lack of harmonized standards.
        <br><br>
        <strong>Key changes proposed:</strong>
        <ul style="margin-top: 0.5rem; margin-left: 1.5rem;">
          <li>High-risk rules apply 6 months after Commission confirms support measures available</li>
          <li>AI literacy obligations shift from companies to Commission/Member States</li>
          <li>Registration requirements for low-risk systems removed</li>
          <li>Permission to process special category data for debiasing AI systems</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

    <hr class="entry-divider">

    <div class="entry-section">
      <div class="entry-section-label">Why It Matters</div>
      <div class="entry-section-content">
        Significant concession to industry pressure. Open letter from 45 leading European companies demanded "two-year clock-stop" - Commission publicly refused but effectively granted 16-month delay through different mechanism.
        <br><br>
        Creates new uncertainty: "Readiness-based application" means rules apply when Commission decides support measures exist - not a fixed date.
      </div>
